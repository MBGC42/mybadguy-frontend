How it works

MyBadGuy reads the limited information your browser makes freely available — your device type and operating system version — then asks you ten short questions about your general situation. No names, no email addresses, no account required.

We run your profile against threat actor models built from publicly available intelligence — FBI IC3 annual reports, FTC Sentinel data, MITRE ATT&CK, and NIST vulnerability databases. Each actor scores your profile based on how attractive you look to them, and why.

The result is a ranked threat profile showing which actors have the most interest in someone like you, and a prioritised list of practical steps to reduce your exposure — specific to your device, your situation, and your risk level.

What we are not

MyBadGuy is an educational awareness tool, not a security scanner. We do not access your files, apps, contacts, camera, microphone, or any device resource beyond what your browser provides automatically. We are not a substitute for professional security advice.
  • Not a vulnerability scanner. We do not probe your device, network, or accounts. Our threat scores are based on your profile characteristics, not live scans.
  • Not a security company. We do not sell security products or professional assessments. We have no financial incentive to inflate your risk score.
  • Not infallible. This site is built with the assistance of artificial intelligence and may occasionally provide information that is inaccurate or out of date. We update our data daily but we are not perfect.
  • Not a substitute for professional advice. For a formal security assessment, consult a qualified cybersecurity professional.

Our data

Every threat score is derived from authoritative public sources — government agencies, academic research, and open standards bodies. We update our databases daily. No proprietary threat intelligence, no vendor bias.

  • CVE data from the NIST National Vulnerability Database — updated every two hours.
  • Actively exploited vulnerabilities from the CISA Known Exploited Vulnerabilities catalog — updated daily.
  • Threat actor profiles from MITRE ATT&CK Enterprise and Mobile — updated weekly.
  • Phishing trends from OpenPhish community feed — updated hourly.
  • OS support lifecycle data from endoflife.date — updated daily.
  • CVE exploitation probability from EPSS (Exploit Prediction Scoring System) — updated daily.
  • Malware and threat intelligence from URLhaus, MalwareBazaar, and ThreatFox (abuse.ch) — updated daily.
  • Ransomware group activity from Ransomware.live — updated weekly.
  • Consumer fraud data from FBI IC3 and FTC Consumer Sentinel annual reports.

See our Data sources page for the full list with links to primary sources.

Our principles

  • Factual. We cite our sources. If we cannot substantiate a claim, we do not make it.
  • Ethical. We will not shame, manipulate, or alarm users to drive engagement. Scores reflect real risk, not marketing.
  • Private. We collect only what is necessary, protect it rigorously, and delete it on request. See our Privacy policy.
  • Transparent. We disclose how our scores are calculated, where our data comes from, and what we do not know.
  • Accessible. Plain language, mobile-first, and free — always. Security awareness should not require a degree or a subscription.
