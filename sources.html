CISA's authoritative catalog of vulnerabilities confirmed as actively exploited in the wild. A CVE in KEV has been weaponized against real targets — not just theoretically possible. We cross-reference every CVE in our database against KEV daily and elevate scores accordingly.

A community-maintained database of OS release cycles, support end dates, and latest version numbers. We use this to determine whether your OS version is current, behind, or end-of-life. An end-of-life OS is treated as fully unpatched — no further security updates will ever be issued.

Updated daily · Covers iOS, iPadOS, Android, macOS, Windows

Exploit Prediction Scoring System (EPSS)
FIRST.org

A machine learning model that estimates the probability of a CVE being exploited within the next 30 days. EPSS complements CVSS severity scores — a CVE can be low severity but high exploitation probability. We weight high-probability CVEs more heavily in tactic signal calculations.

Updated daily via FIRST.org CSV feed · Free
first.org/epss →

Scoring methodology

Two independent scores per actor

Every threat actor produces two sub-scores — a Technical score and a Social score — which are averaged into a Combined score out of 99.

Technical score = base + consumer_boost + device_OS_boost + MDM_boost + location_boost Social score = base + consumer_boost Combined score = round((Technical + Social) / 2)

Device OS boost — powered by NVD + CISA KEV

Each unpatched CVE on your device platform contributes a weighted boost to technical scores. CVEs confirmed in the CISA KEV catalog contribute more than theoretical CVEs. Each actor type has a platform weighting reflecting how much they rely on technical exploits versus social engineering.

What we do not do

We do not scan your device

MyBadGuy does not access your files, contacts, photos, messages, or installed apps. Device detection is limited to what your browser reports — operating system type and version. Everything else is self-reported through the profile questions.

Scores are probabilistic, not diagnostic

A high score means your profile matches characteristics that threat actors target — it does not mean you have been compromised. Scores reflect relative exposure, not confirmed incidents.

We do not use vendor threat intelligence

We do not pay for commercial threat intelligence feeds or third-party data enrichment. Every data source is publicly available and government or open-source in origin.

We do not share your data

We do not share your scan data with advertisers, data brokers, or any third party. Your profile — if you save it — is stored encrypted and only accessible with your private ID and PIN. We cannot access it ourselves.
